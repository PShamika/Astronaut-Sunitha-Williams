    • Sunita Williams

    Sunita Williams, a distinguished American astronaut and retired U.S. Navy officer, has made significant contributions to space exploration, including being the first woman to fly on a flight test of an orbital spacecraft during the 2024 Boeing Crew Flight Test. She has spent a total of 322 days in space and completed nine spacewalks, holding records for the most spacewalk time by a woman.

    Sunita Williams, born Sunita Lyn Pandya on September 19, 1965, in Euclid, Ohio, to an Indian-American father and a Slovenian-American mother, has a strong multicultural background. She graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy in 1987 and became a Naval Aviator, later becoming a NASA astronaut in 1998. Her first space mission involved serving on the International Space Station (ISS) as part of Expeditions 14 and 15, where she spent 195 days in space. She also became the first person to run a marathon in space, completing the Boston Marathon on a treadmill aboard the ISS.

    Williams's career includes multiple spacewalks and commanding the ISS during her second mission, where she also became the first astronaut to complete a triathlon in orbit. She has continued to inspire future astronauts and promotes science and space exploration, serving as an ambassador for getting people interested in these fields.
